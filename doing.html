Be sure to recycle any plastics you use at home or bring to the beach. Always double check you’re packaging and the bin you are disposing of it in to make sure you found the right match.
Along with recycling disposable packaging, why not switch to an eco-friendlier option? Try reusable glass, wood, or metal instead. Also, try biodegradable plastics to decrease the amount of leftover packaging littering the shores.
If you do decide to bring food to the beach, make sure it’s for you, and only you! Don’t disrupt the diets of the animals, such as birds or fishes, that are sharing the shore and sea with you.
Don’t worry, you don’t have to cut root beer out of your diet to help! Simply grab some scissors to cut apart the plastic wrapping of your 6 pack to make sure marine animals don’t get caught in the holes.
If you live near the beach, tag along to a local clean-up. Team up with an organization or plan it yourself!
Want to go a step further? Take a beach under your wing! As an organization, community, or group, you and fellow beach-lovers can adopt an area to regularly maintain and clean up.
Get political! Call or write your local congressman (or woman!) about important issues facing your ocean community. Be the voice for the marine life that can’t speak up for themselves.
If you eat seafood, research the fish you buy and restaurants you eat at too. Make sure you are not contributing to an unethical and unsafe practice.
Buy organic sunscreens or look with a sun protectant with safe ingredients. Keep your skin safe from harm, while also decreasing potential threat to the ocean.
If you bring your pets to the beach, make sure to clean up after them. Even if you are nowhere near the sea, read pet food labels, never flush cat litter down the drain, and don’t mix wild-caught saltwater fish with your aquarium friends (or the other way around).
When you’re planning on taking your trip off-land, be sure to travel the waters responsibly. Research eco-friendly cruise lines, and be a respectful kayaker, canoer, or boater by acknowledging the presence of marine life (don’t throw anything overboard!).
Give money to charity! You can donate to one of our three partners, or another local organization close to your ocean-loving heart. You can also contribute by supporting a marine national park in your area.
Hey, why not talk to your family and friends about all you learned? Educate everyone you love so that they can help do their part to protect the oceans we all love.
